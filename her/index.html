How the Sync Works

Every training day is matched 1:1 with Reshad's Balkans Prep schedule. When he trains lower strength, you train lower strength. When he's on upper pull, you're on upper pull. You'll be at the same section of the gym working the same muscle groups — the difference is load, rest period, and cardio type.

Primary modifications: all barbell compounds are replaced with dumbbell or cable equivalents for better joint tracking at your loading range. HIIT cardio on Lower Glutes and Full Body days is replaced with Zone 2 steady-state — high-cortisol intervals compound water retention at this deficit. Rest periods are 75–90 sec vs his 45–60 sec.

The One-Core-Movement Rule

Each session contains exactly one direct core exercise. This is deliberate and should not be modified. Multiple ab circuits per week create persistent low-grade abdominal inflammation that reads visually as bloat. One movement per session keeps the core trained without the inflammatory response. The compound lower-body work hits the core adequately as a secondary system.

Week-by-Week Progression

Week 1 — Adaptation: Establish the pattern. Do not go to failure on anything. Complete all sessions at the prescribed weights with good form. Note every weight used.

Week 2 — Load: Add 2.5–5 lbs to every exercise where Week 1 felt manageable. Reduce rest by 15 sec. Extend post-workout cardio by 3–5 min.

Week 3 — Output: Match or slightly exceed Week 2 loads through Day 18. From Day 18 onward, taper — last heavy session is Day 18, active recovery only until departure.

Calorie Calculation

At 28 years old, 5'2", 138 lbs, and training 4–5 days/week, estimated TDEE is 1,820–1,870 kcal/day. The 1,475 kcal target creates a daily deficit of roughly 370–400 kcal — aggressive enough to produce visible change in 20 days without crossing below the threshold where muscle catabolism accelerates. Do not go lower than 1,400 on any day.

Active recovery days: Drop to 1,300–1,350 kcal. Training output is lower, so the deficit is maintained without the same fuel requirement. Keep protein at 138g regardless.

Departure day (Jul 2): Eat at maintenance — 1,850 kcal. A single refeed day refills glycogen and firms up muscle tissue for the beach. This is intentional, not a cheat day.

Why 138g Protein Is Non-Negotiable

At this level of caloric deficit combined with 6 training sessions per week, muscle catabolism is the primary risk. Research on aggressive short-term deficits shows that 0.8–1.0g protein per pound of bodyweight is the minimum threshold to preserve lean mass. Falling below this causes the body to catabolize muscle for fuel — which permanently lowers basal metabolic rate and produces a soft, undefined look rather than a toned one.

Hit protein first at every meal, before carbs or fat. If you're short at the end of the day, a scoop of whey isolate in water closes the gap cleanly.

Sample Daily Eating Window (12–8pm)
  • Meal 1 (~12pm, pre-workout): 150g Greek yogurt 0% + 1 scoop whey isolate + 80g oats + blueberries — approx. 55g protein, 55g carbs
  • Post-workout shake (within 30 min): 1 scoop whey isolate + water — approx. 25g protein
  • Meal 2 (~6–7pm, main meal): 180g chicken breast or white fish + 150g cooked white rice or sweet potato + 200g zucchini or spinach + 1 tbsp olive oil — approx. 55g protein, 60g carbs, 15g fat
  • If short on protein: Add 2 eggs or 100g cottage cheese to either meal
Anti-Bloat Food Rules (All 20 Days)
  • Cut immediately: Beans, lentils, chickpeas, raw onion, garlic, broccoli, cauliflower, cabbage, carbonated drinks, artificial sweeteners, deli meats, soft cheeses, alcohol
  • Eat freely: Eggs, chicken breast, white fish, Greek yogurt (plain), white rice, oats, sweet potato, zucchini, cucumber, spinach, blueberries, strawberries, olive oil, avocado (moderate)
  • Sodium: Days 1–13 keep under 1,500 mg/day. Days 14–17 drop to 1,000 mg. Days 18–20 under 800 mg.
  • Water: 2.5–3L days 1–13. Increase to 3.5–4L days 14–17 (water load phase). Taper to 2L days 18–20.
Eating Window Sync with Reshad

Reshad's OMAD window is 5–8pm. Your window is 12–8pm. These overlap from 5–8pm, meaning you can eat dinner together. The difference is you have a midday meal (around training) that he skips. This is intentional — at 138 lbs and a shorter stature, a full OMAD protocol at 1,475 kcal would put your per-meal protein requirement too high to be practical in one sitting.

You: Two cardio blocks totalling 45–50 min. Same core movement. You finish 15–20 min before him — foam roll or stretch while he completes his third block.

RECOVERY
Sleep · Hormonal considerations · Supplements
Sleep — The Cortisol Variable

At this caloric deficit with 6 training sessions per week, sleep quality is the single biggest lever on whether the body looks soft or defined. Under 7 hours, cortisol stays elevated, subcutaneous water retention increases, and the fat loss you are generating gets masked. Target 7.5–8 hours nightly. Finish eating by 8pm. No phone in bed. The 20-min evening walk after the last meal lowers cortisol, reduces GI bloating, and improves sleep onset.

Hormonal Recovery Considerations

If you are in the luteal phase (roughly the two weeks before menstruation) during any part of this 20-day window, perceived exertion will be higher and recovery slower. This is not a reason to skip sessions — it is a reason to use the upper end of your rest period ranges and not push for new weight records during that window. Form and session completion matter more than load.

Iron is also relevant. If fatigue feels disproportionate to training load at any point, prioritize red meat and leafy greens in your eating window. A short iron supplement course is worth considering if that fatigue persists.
