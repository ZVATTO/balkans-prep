Why Visceral Fat Responds First

Visceral fat (the abdominal fat surrounding organs) is metabolically active and releases fatty acids directly into the portal circulation. It has a higher density of beta-adrenergic receptors, making it the first fat depot to respond to catecholamine release during exercise — especially HIIT and fasted cardio.

The single most powerful lever for visceral fat reduction is sustained caloric deficit + elevated cortisol management + HIIT frequency. Insulin suppression from OMAD directly reduces visceral fat accumulation, and the combination with high-volume HIIT creates the ideal hormonal environment for abdominal fat mobilization.

Program Design Rationale

4 strength days per week — preserves muscle mass during aggressive deficit. Without this, up to 40% of weight lost can be lean tissue, which tanks metabolism.

3 HIIT sessions per week — maximizes EPOC (excess post-exercise oxygen consumption). Each HIIT session elevates metabolism for 12–24 hours after training.

Daily cardio — even on strength days. The additional 40–55 min of MISS/LISS adds 300–500 kcal burn without compromising muscle recovery.

Workout timing — train at 2–4pm to be close to your 5pm eating window. Fasted training is fine; breaking fast within 1 hour post-workout is optimal for muscle protein synthesis.

Daily Activity Targets
  • Steps: Week 1: 10,000+ · Week 2: 12,000+ · Week 3: 14,000+
  • Exercise calories burned: 600–1,000 kcal on training days · 250–350 on recovery
  • Non-exercise movement: Take stairs, walk to gym, stand during calls
  • Fasting window: Black coffee, water, electrolytes only until 5pm
  • Eating window: 5–8pm (3 hours). All 1,900 kcal consumed here.
Workout Timing vs. Eating Window

Ideal: Train 2–4pm, break fast at 5pm with your full OMAD meal immediately post-workout. This maximizes muscle protein synthesis and recovery.

If morning training: Take 10g EAAs intra-workout (negligible calories, prevents muscle catabolism in a fasted state). Maintain fast until 5pm with water/black coffee/electrolytes.

Why 185g Protein Is Non-Negotiable

At this level of caloric deficit combined with daily high-volume training, muscle catabolism is your biggest risk. Research on aggressive deficits consistently shows that 0.8–1.0g protein per pound of body weight is the minimum threshold to preserve lean mass. Dropping below this causes your body to cannibalize muscle, which permanently lowers your basal metabolic rate — making long-term fat loss harder.

Hit protein first, every day, before carbs or fat. If you're full and haven't hit 185g, use a protein shake.

Sample OMAD Meal (5–8pm)
  • Protein anchor (185g target): 7–8oz chicken breast or lean ground beef or 6 eggs + 2 egg whites + 200g Greek yogurt (0%)
  • Carbohydrate: 1 cup cooked white rice or 1 medium sweet potato
  • Vegetables (unlimited): Large mixed salad, broccoli, peppers, spinach
  • Fat: ½ avocado or 1 tbsp olive oil on vegetables
  • Optional protein top-up: 1 scoop whey isolate in water if under protein target
Fasting Window (8pm – 5pm)
  • Allowed: Water, black coffee, plain green/black tea, electrolyte powder (0 kcal)
  • Pre-workout if training fasted AM: Black coffee 30 min before, 10g EAAs during training
  • Electrolytes are critical: OMAD + heavy sweating = sodium/potassium/magnesium depletion. Take an electrolyte supplement mid-fast daily.
  • If hunger is severe at 22hr mark: A black coffee or sparkling water is effective. Do not break fast early.
Weekly Calorie Cycling

Training days (Mon–Sat): 1,900 kcal as described above.

Active recovery (Sun): You can drop to 1,600–1,700 kcal since training output is much lower. This accelerates the weekly deficit meaningfully without affecting performance.

Do not drop below 1,600 kcal on any day. Below this threshold for a 198 lb active male, muscle loss accelerates and hormonal disruption (cortisol, testosterone) becomes counterproductive to fat loss.

Sleep — The Most Underrated Fat Loss Tool

Target: 7.5–9 hours nightly. During deep sleep, growth hormone peaks — this is when your body preferentially oxidizes fat for fuel. Poor sleep (under 6 hrs) elevates cortisol by 20–30%, which directly drives visceral fat accumulation and suppresses the fat-burning hormonal environment you're working to create through training.

Practical: finish eating by 8pm, lights dim by 10pm, no phone in bed. Sleep is when this plan pays off.

Hydration
  • Daily minimum: 3.5–4 litres of water
  • During training: 500–750ml intra-workout
  • Electrolytes: One serving electrolyte powder (LMNT, Ultima, or similar) mid-fasting window daily — critical given OMAD + sweating
  • Sodium: Don't over-restrict. Low sodium + high water = hyponatremia risk during heavy training. A pinch of sea salt in your water bottle is fine.
Supplements
Creatine Monohydrate
5g/day
Preserves lean mass in caloric deficit. Improves high-intensity output. Slight water retention in muscle (not subcutaneous — does not affect appearance). Take with your OMAD meal.
Whey Protein Isolate
30–40g as needed
Use only to hit your 185g protein target if food sources fall short. Isolate preferred (lower carbs/fat). Include in OMAD window.
EAAs (Essential Amino Acids)
10g intra-workout
Only necessary if training more than 16hrs into your fast (AM training). Prevents fasted muscle catabolism without breaking the fast meaningfully.
Caffeine / Black Coffee
200–400mg pre-workout
Enhances fat oxidation when fasted. Improves performance. Natural coffee or caffeine tablets both work. Cut off by 2pm to protect sleep.
Electrolytes
1 serving/day
Sodium, potassium, magnesium. Critical for OMAD protocol. Prevents fatigue, cramping, and the mid-fast energy crashes that derail training.
Omega-3 (Fish Oil)
2–3g EPA/DHA
Anti-inflammatory. Supports cortisol regulation. Emerging evidence for direct visceral fat reduction. Take with OMAD meal.
Vitamin D3 + K2
3,000–5,000 IU D3
Most people training hard in Canada are deficient. Supports testosterone, immune function, and bone health. Take with OMAD meal (fat-soluble).
Magnesium Glycinate
300–400mg
Take 30 min before sleep. Improves sleep quality measurably. Also replenishes magnesium lost through sweat during high-volume training.
Daily Recovery Protocol
  • Post-workout: 5–10 min foam rolling (calves, quads, lats, thoracic spine)
  • Sunday active recovery: 10–15 min full body stretch + foam roll session
  • Cold exposure (optional): 2–3 min cold shower post-workout reduces DOMS and inflammation, supports cortisol clearance
  • Stress management: Cortisol is your enemy for visceral fat. Keep non-training stressors low these 20 days where possible
  • Alcohol: Avoid entirely. Alcohol stops fat oxidation, disrupts sleep architecture, and adds empty calories outside your OMAD window
